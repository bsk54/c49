###
  1. Tones & Characters

    Chinese has thousands of written characters, and each character corresponds to a single spoken syllable, which in turn can carry up to four tones—each tone changing its meaning. As a result, students typically take two more years to learn Chinese than Western or Central European languages.

    Check out online resources such as YouTube, TikTok, or Instagram to learn the basics of the four-tone system and the fundamentals of the writing system.

    In the meantime, start memorizing the Chinese characters presented here as if they were images you want to remember.

    Remember today (!) that tone pronunciation has two key exceptions:

    1. Two 3rd-tones in a row → first becomes 2nd tone
      • 你好 (written nǐ hǎo | hello) → pronounced ní hǎo
      • 很好 (written hěn hǎo | very good) → pronounced hén hǎo
    2. The word 不 (bù - 4th tone | not) before another 4th tone → becomes 2nd tone
      • 不是 | bú shì | Is not
###
  1. Pronouns: 我 / 你 / 他
    • 我 (wǒ | I / me)
    • 你 (nǐ | you)
    • 他 (tā | he / him)
    • 她 (tā | she / her)
    Examples:
    • 我很好。
      (Wǒ hěn hǎo. | I’m fine. I’m very good.)
    • 你好吗？
      (Nǐ hǎo ma? | How are you?)
    • 他不好。
      (Tā bù hǎo | He is not good)
###
  1. The Copula 是 (shì | to be)
    Use 是 to link two nouns or pronouns.
    • 我是老师
      (Wǒ shì lǎoshī | I am a teacher)
    • 我不是你
      (Wǒ bú shì nǐ | I am not you)
    Note: 是 does not express location. To say “to be at [somewhere]”, use 在 (zài) instead.
    • 我在家
      (Wǒ zài jiā | I am at home)
###
  1. Negation with 不 (not)
    Put 不 (bù | not) before verbs or adjectives (present/future). Examples:
    • 他不好。
      (Tā bù hǎo. | He is not good.)
    • 你不要走。
      (Nǐ bù yào zǒu. | Don’t go.)
###
  1. Yes/No Questions with 吗
    Add 吗 (ma | question particle) to the end of a statement. Examples:
    • 你是老师吗？
      (Nǐ shì lǎoshī ma? | Are you a teacher?)
    • 他不好吗？
      (Tā bù hǎo ma? | Isn’t he okay?)
